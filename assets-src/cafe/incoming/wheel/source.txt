Modifications (by this project)
-------------------------------
- Rescaled to a real-world diameter of 0.26 m, oriented standing upright with
  the grip/driver face toward -X, bottom-most point grounded at Z=0, origin at
  footprint center.
- Trademark scrub (public repo): removed the "HONDA" engine wordmark and its
  "H" emblem from the center pad (base-colour texture). No other sponsor marks
  (McLaren / Marlboro / Momo) were present in the textures or geometry after
  inspection of all three maps; the coloured switch caps carry no wordmarks and
  were left as period-correct switchgear. The removed logo area was filled with
  cloned suede-grain from the surrounding pad. No text or logos remain anywhere.
- The period-correct three-spoke suede/leather wheel character is retained
  (suede rim, satin metal button rims, recessed centre boss).
- A small subtle display stand was added — two minimal dark metal support posts
  under the lower rim — because a thin wheel disc cannot plausibly stand on its
  own on a shelf.
- Material handling: the source uses packed ORM (metallic-roughness) + normal
  maps routed through a Separate Color node (G→roughness, B→metallic); this
  routing was verified correct and retained, so the suede reads matte and the
  metal hardware reads satin. No emission leaks.
- Compressed for the web: meshopt geometry compression + WebP textures via
  @gltf-transform/cli (Draco not used).

Destination: GT Café display exhibit (public/models/cafe-wheel.glb)
